Robe and hem rules:
- If the source is a robe, ankle-length garment, floor-length garment, or skirt-like lower garment that goes well past the knees, convert it into a full ground-length robe.
- The bottom must be capped: flatten the hem into a simple horizontal or very slightly curved ground-contact shape.
- The hem should touch the ground plane with a visible contact shadow.
- Do not show feet, boots, toes, heels, or separate legs under a ground-length robe.
- Do not show thighs, knees, calves, or a full mannequin leg through a ground-length robe opening.
- Do not create high slits, open front gaps, parted robe panels, or robe openings that reveal the lower body.
- If the source has exposed lower-body skin or leg openings, close the robe panels and preserve the design through trim, color, seams, or layered cloth instead.
- Do not create open interior cavities under the robe.
- Do not let the image-to-3D viewer see inside the robe from below.
- Keep the lower robe as a clean closed volume, like a capped garment shell.
- Avoid ragged trailing strips, loose dangling cloth, or wind-blown edges.

If the source has a shorter tunic or knee-length garment, keep it as a shorter tunic only when it ends around the knees or above. If it clearly extends significantly below the knees, make it a full ground-length robe with hidden feet and a capped bottom.

Belts, sashes, collars, cuffs, decorative trim, broad cloth panels, and robe sleeves are allowed. Keep them broad, clean, and PBR-readable.
